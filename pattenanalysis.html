SECTION 6: PRIORITY LIST

To maximize efficiency, structure your revision based on weightage, scoring probability, and historical consistency.

Priority 1: Must Do (High Yield, Predictable)

  • Ray Optics & Wave Optics: High weightage (approx. 14-18 marks combined). Derivations and diagrams are highly predictable.
  • Semiconductor Electronics: Extremely scoring. Straightforward theory (Rectifiers, V-I characteristics).
  • Current Electricity: Kirchhoff's laws numericals and drift velocity derivations are guaranteed appearances.
  • Modern Physics (Dual Nature, Atoms, Nuclei): Formula-based numericals and direct theoretical questions. Very high return on time invested.

Priority 2: Important (Requires Conceptual Depth)

  • Electrostatics (Ch 1 & 2): Heavy on concepts. Gauss's law and capacitor circuits require solid understanding.
  • Alternating Current: Phasor diagrams and resonance conditions are frequent but can be mathematically intensive for some.
  • Moving Charges and Magnetism: Biot-Savart and Ampere's law derivations are critical.

Priority 3: Less Important (Low Weightage / Highly Variable)

  • Electromagnetic Waves: Only 2-3 marks. Memorize the EM spectrum, uses, and the displacement current concept.
  • Magnetism and Matter: Mostly definitions (dia/para/ferro) and basic Earth's magnetism concepts.
  • Electromagnetic Induction: Aside from Motional EMF and Lenz's law, questions here often merge with AC, making isolated EMI questions less frequent.
