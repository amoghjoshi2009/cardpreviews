CBSE does not publish any official “prediction” or exact frequency table of questions. Publicly available data include past papers, the official sample paper and marking scheme, the official Competency‑Focused Practice Questions, and blueprints / weightage tables from CBSE and major publishers.
From these, clear patterns emerge, but exact “frequency counts” and “probability percentages” for each derivation or question are **not** officially available. Any such numbers in coaching material are private estimates, not CBSE data.

So the answer below is a *trend‑based, evidence‑backed prediction*, not a guarantee. Where other sources quote specific years (e.g., “asked in 2019, 2021, 2023”), those are cited; otherwise, probabilities are expressed qualitatively (Very High / High / Medium / Low) rather than fake percentages.

## STEP 3 – CBSE Trend Analysis (Which chapters dominate, rotate, were strong/weak in 2025)

### Chapters consistently favored every year

From official weightage tables (Oswal, LearnCBSE, Shiksha, PW, etc.) and analysis of 2015–2025 papers, the *core* units that always carry substantial marks are:

- **Electrostatics + Current Electricity (Units I & II)** – combined ~16 marks.
- **Magnetism + EMI + AC (Units III & IV)** – combined ~17 marks.
- **Optics (Unit VI)** – about 14 marks within an 18‑mark block with EM waves.
- **Modern Physics (Dual Nature, Atoms, Nuclei) (Units VII & VIII)** – about 12 marks.
- **Semiconductor Devices (Unit IX)** – ~7 marks.

These units *dominate the conceptual and numerical load* of the paper.

### Chapters “rotated” mainly in style, not in presence

- **Electromagnetic Waves (Ch 8)**: Often appears as 1‑mark MCQ/AR + sometimes 2‑mark conceptual; rarely long numericals.
- **Magnetism & Matter (Ch 5)**: Some years mostly theory (properties of materials, earth’s field), other years appears inside numericals (magnetic dipole, BH curve).
- **Wave Optics (Ch 10)**: Sometimes gets full 3–5 mark derivation (YDSI/diffraction), other years more MCQ/short conceptual.

So, almost *every* chapter appears every year; what rotates is whether it gets a heavy derivation/numerical versus small MCQs/shorts.

### What 2025 board paper emphasized

From 2025 analysis and PYQ pages:

- **High emphasis:**
- Electrostatics & Current Electricity – conceptual + numericals.
- Magnetism & EMI/AC – several tricky numericals and reasoning.
- **Optics** – including ray optics derivation (lens maker / lens formula) and instruments.
- **Modern Physics (Dual Nature + Atoms + Nuclei)** – derivation/numerical combo; photoelectric effect and nuclear Qs.

- **EM Waves and basic theory topics** appeared largely as direct MCQs and short conceptual questions – comparatively “easy scoring”.

### Likely shifts for 2026 (based on blueprint + sample paper + 2026 derivation lists)

- The **official 2025‑26 SQP** already locks in:
- A **big 5‑mark derivation in Optics** (Lens maker) AND
- A **5‑mark electromagnetism or EMI/AC derivation** (transformer / galvanometer conversion) AND
- A **5‑mark Electrostatics/Capacitance derivation**.

- Coaching analyses and articles for “top derivations 2026” emphasize **exactly the same cluster**: Gauss law & dipole fields, capacitor with dielectric, Ohm’s law from drift, force between parallel wires, lens maker, prism deviation, Bohr model, Einstein PE equation, rectifiers.

Given CBSE’s policy of keeping the *assessment scheme unchanged* for 2025‑26 and the official sample reflecting the internal blueprint, it is highly likely that **Electrostatics, Circuits, Magnetism/EMI/AC, Optics, and Modern Physics will again dominate 2026.**

### SECTION A (16 × 1 = 16 marks)

**Q1–12: MCQs (one correct)**

1. A short electric dipole is placed along +x axis at origin. At a distant point P on x‑axis, electric field is \(E_0\). If the dipole moment is doubled keeping length same, the field at P becomes:
(A) \(E_0/2\) (B) \(E_0\) (C) \(2E_0\) (D) \(4E_0\)

2. The net electric flux through a closed Gaussian surface depends on:
(A) charge outside the surface only
(B) charge enclosed by the surface only
(C) both enclosed and external charge
(D) shape and size of the surface

3. A dielectric slab is inserted fully between the plates of an isolated charged parallel plate capacitor. Which quantity remains unchanged?
(A) Capacitance (B) Electric field between plates
(C) Charge on plates (D) Energy stored

4. In a conductor carrying steady current:
(A) the drift velocity of electrons is zero
(B) the electric field inside is zero
(C) the charge density is zero
(D) number of electrons crossing any cross‑section per second is constant

5. Two long parallel wires carry equal currents in the same direction. The force between them is:
(A) attractive and inversely proportional to distance
(B) repulsive and inversely proportional to distance
(C) attractive and inversely proportional to square of distance
(D) repulsive and directly proportional to distance

6. An EM wave in vacuum has electric field \(E_0 = 6\) V/m. The amplitude of accompanying magnetic field is:
(A) \(2 \times 10^{-8}\) T (B) \(2 \times 10^{-7}\) T
(C) \(2 \times 10^{-9}\) T (D) \(6 \times 10^{-8}\) T

7. The pair of electromagnetic radiations arranged in increasing order of frequency is:
(A) radio, X‑ray, microwave
(B) microwave, infrared, UV
(C) infrared, microwave, gamma
(D) UV, visible, radio

8. A convex lens of focal length 20 cm forms an image of an object at 40 cm from lens on the other side. The magnification is:
(A) 1/2 (B) 1 (C) 2 (D) –2

9. In Young’s double slit experiment, the central bright fringe is always:
(A) coloured (B) dark
(C) white/bright (D) absent

10. According to Einstein’s photoelectric equation, a graph of KEmax vs frequency is:
(A) straight line with negative slope
(B) hyperbola
(C) straight line with positive slope
(D) parabola

11. The radius of nth orbit of hydrogen atom is proportional to:
(A) n (B) n² (C) 1/n (D) 1/n²

12. In a forward biased silicon diode, approximate potential barrier across junction is:
(A) 0.1 V (B) 0.3 V (C) 0.7 V (D) 1.2 V

**Q13–16: Assertion–Reason type**

13. Assertion (A): Electric field inside a hollow conducting shell is zero everywhere.
Reason (R): Charge resides only on the outer surface of a hollow conductor.

14. Assertion (A): Drift velocity of electrons in a conductor is of the order of mm/s.
Reason (R): Potential difference across conductor produces an electric field, which exerts a force on free electrons.

15. Assertion (A): In single slit diffraction, the central maximum is wider than other maxima.
Reason (R): Condition for first minimum is \(a \sin \theta = \lambda\), where a is slit width.

16. Assertion (A): In a full‑wave rectifier, ripple factor is less than in a half‑wave rectifier.
Reason (R): In full‑wave rectifier, current flows through load during both half cycles of AC.
